French Onion Soup



Description


French Onion Soup is a classic by any definition. This soup is packed full of the flavours of caramelized onion, rich beef stock, crispy baguette, and topped with gooey cheese. A great French onion soup is not difficult to make but the one thing you do need is time. But this classic of French cookery is more than worth it.


Ingredients

  • 3 lbs onion yellow onion - sliced thinly
  • 6 tbsp butter
  • Kosher salt and fresh ground pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup sherry
  • 2 quart beef stock
  • 2 sprigs thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tsp cider vinegar
  • Sliced baugette
  • 1 lb Gruyere cheese, grated

Directions

  1. Melt butter in a large pot and then begin sweating all the onions in the pot. After sweating onion redude temp to medium and begin the process of browning onions over the next couple of hours

  2. After onions are caramelized deglaze the pan with sherry and burn off the alcohol smell
  3. Add stock and thyme and bay leaf, bring to a boil then reduce to a simmer for 30 minutes
  4. Discard bay leaf and thyme and add cider vinegar, season with salt and papper
  5. Fill soup dishes with soup and top with slices of toasted baugette
  6. Top with grated cheese and then place under oven broiler until cheese is melted and browned
  7. Remove from oven once cheese is browned and top with chives if desired and server
  8. Bon Appetit!
